Ruby on Rails Camp

Ruby on Rails Camp was a gathering of enthusiasts who wanted to share and learn more about Ruby on Rails (RoR) in an open environment. It was an intense event with discussions, demos, and interaction from participants.

The Ruby on Rails Camp Was a Success!

The camp is over now, but you can find out more about it:

Focus

The theme was “Is Ruby on Rails Ready for Business?” and focused on using RoR for real business applications.

Unconference

Held in the Unconference style, there were no pre-planned speakers or agendas. As a first agenda item, there was a call for sessions that the participants themselves gave. So everyone came prepared to share what they knew!

There was also a Speed Demo session to show off our RoR applications. There was 5 minutes to present our application before the horn sounded and the groups moved on. The applications weren’t all commercially polished; there were a lot of hacks, gems and utilities too!

When and Where

The conference was on November 9th, 2006 at IBM Almaden. IBM also generously provided breakfast, lunch, and snacks, and there was a coffee barista for free coffee needs. We also had a free sushi dinner afterwards which provided great networking opportunities.

How Many

We first planned for 60, but that filled up in a few days. We then opened it up to 120, but even that filled up quickly. So we then recalculated and figured we could squeeze in about 150 people, and that was about how many attended.
