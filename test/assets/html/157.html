Welcome to the Ruby Valley

This website is still under construction but please look around.  There is plenty of information regarding activities and resources in the Ruby Valley.  The Community Calendar is full of news on upcoming events in the Ruby Valley.  The Interactive Map is 99% complete and will provide you with information on our local landmarks and natural playgrounds.  The Notable History of the Ruby Valley has lots of great information and vintage photos.  Our area has such a wealth of history, this page may never been truly complete.  The Community Profile has information on our local schools, organizations, employment and new business resources and much more.  The Business Directory is ready for your perusal.  Our County Extension Agent, Andrea Sarchet, is working on materials to keep our agricultural community up-to-date on important issues and she will be posting to the Aggie Weblog shortly.  Our Visitor Information pages will get more attention as soon as hunting season and the holidays are behind us.  We'll have plenty of details on the wonderful fishing, hunting, camping, hiking and other outdoor activities to be found in our corner of Montana.  

When we're done, we hope this website will be provide a virtual impression of the enchanting beauty and country style of the Ruby Valley for potential visitors.  We also endeavor that it will be used as a reference location for our community to provide information on local events and businesses.

If you would like to contact the Chamber to request information about the Ruby Valley, offer suggestions and provide additional information about the local history, geography, and activities, please click on the Communications link at the bottom of the page.  You'll need to register with an e-mail address confirmation and login to obtain our contact information.  We apologize for the process but this system provides us with a secure, spam-free, hassle-free means of e-mail communication.

Please click here for the registration form. 

Ruby Dam Project - Les Gilman made a short presentation at the latest Chamber meeting regarding the project.  If the dam is not repaired, the DNRC may require that the reservoir not be completely filled.  This would have a HUGE impact on our ranchers, recreationists and outfitters, especially at the end of the summer.  Insufficient water in the Ruby River would cost our communities millions of dollars.  Governor Schweitzer will be receiving federal funds as part of the federal bailout package and a portion of those can be used to fix our dam.  It is up to the Governor's office to allocate the federal bailout funds.  Please contact Governor Schweitzer and let him know that you would appreciate his approval of the Ruby Dam Restoration Project.   The easiest means of communicating your concerns is to go to http://governor.mt.gov/, in the left column click on "Send Comments".  An on-line form will appear.  Please use it to express your concerns regarding this project.  Please be assured that these comments do not go into a digital recyle bin.  Your comments will be read and you may even receive a reply from the Governor himself!  A few minutes of your time will help get this "shovel-ready" project back on track.
